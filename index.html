Emerging Strategy: our vision is to have touchpoints across the site dedicated as spaces for experimentation. Resident and visiting inventors, artists, makers and growers will be creating and installing innovations in all areas of the farm including pastures and the dairy barn.

Our range of public focused activities will include immersive educational experiences, presentations, hands on workshops, and inventors’ demonstrations. Events, such as farm to table dinners, community gatherings, and cultural activities will be ongoing.


share

Bring your project to the farm. Stay the night or longer. Our live /work program offers onsite housing and fabrication opportunities to regenerative practitioners and farmers working on early stage innovations in our four focus areas: farming, energy, shelter and waste.


land

Newledo is an 18 acre farm on the Oregon Coast. The site has wide open pastures, two creeks, a pond and wetland areas. We’re currently engaged in restoration planning to create improved habitat for salmon and beaver. We plan to be open to the public, through tours focused on habitat restoration and watershed health.

who are we?

Nicole Cousino is an entrepreneur, regenerative practioner and creative strategist. Her background includes mission based startups, documentary film, industrial design, permaculture and sustainable sanitation. Nicole collaborated on a ground breaking waste grease-to-biodiesel project in the mid 90s. This led to being the lead researcher on a trap grease-to-biodiesel startup in Philadelphia. Her creative work in video and installation has shown internationally. She’s passionate about meaningful impact in response to our climate and environmental crisis.

Richard Haskell is a web developer, industrial designer, maker and the network tech here at Newledo. His work is informed by his ongoing interest in physics, AI, appropriate technology and by concepts of both functionalism and panpsycism. He is an advocate for alternative transit, an avid bicyclist and outdoor adventurer.
